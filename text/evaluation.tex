\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

We benchmark six LLMs with total parameters spanning 8B to 120B: three dense (Llama-3.1-8B, Qwen3.5-9B, Qwen3.5-27B) and three mixture-of-experts (Mixtral-8$\times$7B, gpt-oss-20b, gpt-oss-120b). All models are served on H100-SXM5-80GB (1$\times$, 2$\times$, and 4$\times$ GPU configurations) and A100-SXM4-40GB (1$\times$, 2$\times$, 4$\times$, and 8$\times$) GPUs using vLLM v0.19 in bf16 precision with prefix caching enabled; A100 and SGLang comparisons are in Appendix~\ref{sec:appendix_hw}. We sweep concurrency from 1 to 320, issuing 50 requests after a 3-request warmup per configuration. Section~\ref{sec:setup} details the experimental setup, Section~\ref{sec:chat_vs_agentic} compares Tier~2 chat and Tier~1 agentic workloads, Section~\ref{sec:single_vs_multi} examines single-turn and multi-turn sessions, and Section~\ref{sec:kernel_roofline} traces performance differences to kernel-level regime shifts.

\paragraph{Cross-GPU generalisation.}
The present per-kernel predictor is trained on A100 ncu data only. H100 ncu profiling was blocked during the submission window by per-pod counter-access permissions on the cloud GPU provider we used, so an H100-specific per-kernel predictor is deferred to a subsequent revision. The A100-only numbers above are not expected to transfer unchanged to other GPUs --- cuBLAS dispatches different kernel variants, FlashAttention backends differ (Hopper TMA vs Ampere), and launch overhead scales differently. Fitting a separate per-(kernel\_family, GPU, backend) predictor, as Section~\ref{sec:perop_method} outlines, is the intended path.
